### Adjective Endings — Two Positions

German adjectives behave differently depending on where they appear in a sentence.

#### Position 1 — Predicate Adjective (after "sein")
When an adjective comes **after** the verb *sein* (to be), it has **no ending**:

| Example | Translation |
|---------|-------------|
| Der Mann ist **groß**. | The man is tall. |
| Die Frau ist **groß**. | The woman is tall. |
| Das Kind ist **groß**. | The child is tall. |

---

#### Position 2 — Attributive Adjective (before a noun)
When an adjective comes **before** a noun, it must take an **ending** that matches the noun's gender and case.

##### With Definite Article (der/die/das) — Nominative

| Gender | Example | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Masculine | Der **groß**e Mann heißt Peter. | The tall man is called Peter. |
| Feminine | Die **groß**e Frau ist sehr nett. | The tall woman is very nice. |
| Neuter | Das **groß**e Kind ist laut. | The big child is loud. |

##### With Indefinite Article (ein/eine/ein) — Nominative

| Gender | Example | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Masculine | Ein **groß**er Mann | A tall man |
| Feminine | Eine **groß**e Frau | A tall woman |
| Neuter | Ein **groß**es Kind | A big child |
| Plural | **Groß**e Kinder | Big children |

##### With Definite Article — Accusative

| Gender | Example | Translation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Masculine | Ich sehe einen **groß**en Mann. | I see a tall man. |
| Feminine | Ich sehe eine **groß**e Frau. | I see a tall woman. |
| Neuter | Ich sehe ein **groß**es Kind. | I see a big child. |

### Adjectives with Colors — Clothing Examples

#### Indefinite Article (ein/eine) — Accusative
| German | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| Susanne trägt einen **blau**en Rock. | Susanne is wearing a blue skirt. |
| Susanne trägt eine **blau**e Bluse. | Susanne is wearing a blue blouse. |
| Sie trägt ein **blau**es T-Shirt. | She is wearing a blue T-shirt. |

#### Definite Article (den/die/das) — Accusative
| German | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| Susanne trägt den **blau**en Rock, den ihre Oma ihr geschenkt hat. | Susanne is wearing the blue skirt her grandma gave her. |
| Susanne trägt die **blau**e Bluse, die ihre Oma ihr geschenkt hat. | Susanne is wearing the blue blouse her grandma gave her. |
| Susanne trägt das **blau**e T-Shirt, das ihre Oma ihr geschenkt hat. | Susanne is wearing the blue T-shirt her grandma gave her. |

### Adjective Ending Summary Table

| | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|-|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **NOM** — definite (der/die/das) | -e | -e | -e | -en |
| **NOM** — indefinite (ein/eine/ein) | -**er** | -e | -**es** | -e |
| **AKK** — definite (den/die/das) | -**en** | -e | -e | -en |
| **AKK** — indefinite (einen/eine/ein) | -**en** | -e | -**es** | -e |

---

### Grammar Tips

> **Predicate vs. attributive:** The position of the adjective determines whether it needs an ending:
> - *Der Mann ist groß.* — no ending (predicate, after *sein*)
> - *Der groß**e** Mann…* — ending required (attributive, before noun)

> **The definite article "does the heavy lifting":** When using *der/die/das*, the article already shows the gender clearly, so the adjective ending is simply **-e** or **-en**. When using *ein/eine*, the article is less informative (ein could be masculine or neuter), so the adjective ending must compensate and show more information (-**er**, -**es**).

> **Color adjectives follow the same rules** as all other adjectives — always match to the gender and case of the noun they describe.
