## 2.2 Articles & Cases (Artikel & Fälle)

### Definite Articles ("the") — Direct Articles

| | Masculine (m) | Feminine (f) | Neuter (n) | Plural (pl) |
|-|--------------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| **Nominative** | **der** | **die** | **das** | **die** |
| **Accusative** | **den** | die | das | die |

> Only the **masculine** article changes between Nominative and Accusative: *der → den*

---

### Indefinite Articles ("a/an") — Indirect Articles

| | Masculine (m) | Feminine (f) | Neuter (n) | Plural (pl) |
|-|--------------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| **Nominative** | **ein** | **eine** | **ein** | *(none)* |
| **Accusative** | **einen** | eine | ein | *(none)* |

> Again, only the **masculine** form changes: *ein → einen*

---

### Quick Reference: Common Nouns by Gender

| German | Gender | Definite | Indefinite |
|--------|--------|----------|------------|
| Hund (dog) | masculine | der Hund | ein Hund |
| Katze (cat) | feminine | die Katze | eine Katze |
| Haus (house) | neuter | das Haus | ein Haus |

### Cases — Overview

German uses **cases** to show the role a noun plays in a sentence.

#### Nominative (NOM) — the Subject
The nominative is used for the **subject** of the sentence (the person/thing doing the action).

| Example | Translation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Der** Vater ist nett. | The father is nice. |
| **Die** Mutter ist groß. | The mother is tall. |
| **Das** Kind ist laut. | The child is loud. |
| Das ist **ein** Hund. | That is a dog. |

#### Accusative (AKK) — the Direct Object
The accusative is used for the **direct object** (the person/thing receiving the action).

| Example | Translation |
|---------|-------------|
| Ich sehe **den** Vater. | I see the father. |
| Ich sehe **die** Mutter. | I see the mother. |
| Ich sehe **das** Kind. | I see the child. |
| Hast du **einen** Hund? | Do you have a dog? |
| Ich sehe **einen** großen Mann. | I see a tall man. |

---

### Negation with Nouns — kein / keine

> Use **kein/keine/kein** to negate nouns (meaning "no" or "not a"). It follows the same pattern as *ein/eine/ein* but also has a plural form.

| | Masculine (m) | Feminine (f) | Neuter (n) | Plural (pl) |
|-|--------------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| | **kein** | **keine** | **kein** | **keine** |

| Example | Translation |
|---------|-------------|
| Das ist **kein** Bruder. | That is not a brother. |
| Das ist **keine** Schwester. | That is not a sister. |
| Das ist **kein** Baby. | That is not a baby. |
| Sind das Eltern? Nein, das sind **keine** Eltern. | Are those parents? No, those are not parents. |
| Ich habe **kein** Haus. | I don't have a house. |
| Ich habe **keine** Haustiere. | I have no pets. |

#### Accusative forms of kein:
| Example | Translation |
|---------|-------------|
| Hast du einen Bruder? Nein, ich habe **keinen** Bruder. | Do you have a brother? No, I have no brother. |
| Hast du eine Schwester? Nein, ich habe **keine** Schwester. | No, I have no sister. |
| Isst du einen Apfel? Nein, ich esse **keinen** Apfel. | No, I'm not eating an apple. |

### Example Sentences — Putting It Together

| German | Translation | Note |
|--------|-------------|------|
| Das ist **der** Vater. | This is the father. | NOM — subject |
| Ich sehe **den** Vater. | I see the father. | AKK — direct object |
| Hast du **einen** Hund? | Do you have a dog? | AKK — direct object |
| Ist das **eine** Katze? Nein, das ist **keine** Katze! | Is that a cat? No, that's not a cat! | NOM — negation with kein |
| Ich habe **keinen** Bruder. | I don't have a brother. | AKK — negation with kein |

---

### Grammar Tips

> **Only masculine changes in accusative:** Feminine, neuter, and plural articles look the same in both nominative and accusative. Only *der → den* and *ein → einen*.

> **nicht vs. kein:**
> - Use **nicht** to negate **verbs** and adjectives: *Ich schwimme nicht.*
> - Use **kein/keine/kein** to negate **nouns**: *Ich habe keine Katze.*

> **Memorize gender with the noun:** German gender is often unpredictable, so always learn the article alongside the word:
> - ✅ *der Hund, die Katze, das Haus*
> - ❌ Don't learn just: *Hund, Katze, Haus*
