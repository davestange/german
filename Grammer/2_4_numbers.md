### 21–29 (and beyond)

> For compound numbers (21–99), German puts the **ones digit first**, joined by **-und-**:
> *ones + und + tens* → e.g., **einundzwanzig** (one-and-twenty = 21)

### Grammar Tips

> **Ones before tens:** In German, compound numbers are built in reverse order compared to English:
> - English: twenty-**one** (tens first)
> - German: **ein**undzwanzig (ones first)
> This applies to all numbers from 21–99.

> **dreißig** (30) is spelled with **-ß-**, not *dreissig* or *dreizzig* — this is one to memorize!

> **No space, one word:** Compound numbers are always written as a single word:
> - *siebenundzwanzig* (not *sieben und zwanzig*)
